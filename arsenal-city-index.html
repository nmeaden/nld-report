📊 Overview & Head-to-Head

57 Premier League meetings since August 1992. Arsenal hold the all-time PL edge — but the picture is far more nuanced. The first 15 years belonged emphatically to Arsenal; the following 15 swung decisively to City; the last three have been fiercely contested between genuine title rivals.

🕰️ Era Analysis

1992–96
Early PL
Arsenal utterly dominant
W6 D2 L0 · 8 games · City struggled in the top flight before relegation in 1996. Arsenal won all six decisive games, conceding just 3 goals. City scored one PL goal vs Arsenal in four seasons — a 1-2 defeat at Highbury.
2000–08
Arsenal's Peak
Arsenal continue to dominate
W11 D1 L2 · 14 games · The Invincibles era and beyond. City returned to the PL (briefly in 2000-01, then from 2002 onwards), and Arsenal hammered them 9-0 across two games on their return. The 5-3 in Feb 2003 and back-to-back Invincibles wins complete a dominant stretch.
2008–16
Transition
Power gradually shifting to City
W4 D4 L8 · 16 games · The Abu Dhabi takeover in 2008 transformed City. The Adebayor celebration game (Sep 2009, 4-2), the 6-3 demolition (Dec 2013), and City's first title in 44 years (2011-12) mark City's rise. Arsenal still competitive but the balance tips.
2016–22
City Dominant
Guardiola era — City's zenith
W0 D0 L12 · 12 games · Arsenal won zero PL points against City across six seasons. City outscored Arsenal 27-2 in their home games during this stretch. The 5-0 at the Etihad (Aug 2021) summed up Arsenal's helplessness. City won four PL titles.
2022–25
Title Rivals
Arsenal back at the table
W4 D3 L3 · 10 games (incl. 25/26) · Arsenal ended the 12-game winless run with a 1-0 win in Oct 2023 — the first PL win since Dec 2015. The 5-1 in Feb 2025 was Arsenal's biggest win in the modern era. Arsenal led both the 2022-23 and 2023-24 title races before City pipped them. The pendulum is swinging back.

💥 Iconic Matches

Oct 2000 · Highbury · Arsenal 5–0 Manchester City
Arsenal's biggest home win in this fixture. City had just been promoted back to the PL for the first time since 1996. Completely outclassed — Thierry Henry, Robert Pires and Sylvain Wiltord among the scorers.
Feb 2003 · Maine Road · Man City 1–5 Arsenal
The last game ever played at Maine Road. Arsenal's biggest away win in the fixture. City's final match at their historic ground ended in humiliation. Henry was imperious.
Sep 2009 · City of Manchester Stadium · Man City 4–2 Arsenal
Emmanuel Adebayor — sold from Arsenal that summer — scored and ran the entire length of the pitch to celebrate in front of the Arsenal fans. One of the most provocative moments in this fixture's history. City's first major statement of post-takeover intent.
Dec 2013 · Etihad Stadium · Man City 6–3 Arsenal
The record winning margin in this PL fixture and the most goals ever in a single match between the two sides. Aguero, Yaya Touré and Samir Nasri (ex-Arsenal) among the scorers. Arsenal had been top of the league going in.
Feb 2018 · Wembley · Arsenal 0–3 Manchester City (EFL Cup Final)
The first competitive cup final between the clubs. City were clinical — Agüero, Kompany and David Silva. Arsenal were outclassed. Days later City beat Arsenal 3-0 in the league. Wenger's last season ended without silverware.
Aug 2021 · Etihad Stadium · Man City 5–0 Arsenal
Granit Xhaka sent off after 35 minutes. Completely one-sided. Arsenal's worst result in this fixture in the modern era. At this point City had won 11 consecutive PL meetings. Arsenal had not won a PL game at City since 2015.
Oct 2023 · Emirates Stadium · Arsenal 1–0 Manchester City
Gabriel Martinelli's 86th-minute deflected shot ended Arsenal's 12-game PL winless run against City (dating back to December 2015). A statement result in the middle of a genuine title challenge. The first PL win for Arsenal against City in eight years.
Feb 2025 · Emirates Stadium · Arsenal 5–1 Manchester City
Arsenal's biggest win against City in the modern era. Myles Lewis-Skelly scored his first senior goal to make it 3-1. City were in freefall. A moment that crystalised how much the balance of power has shifted in Arteta's favour.

Winning Streaks

Arsenal: 8 consecutive PL wins (1992–2008 overall; in the early PL era Arsenal went long stretches unbeaten). Notable 4-game winning run in 2000-04 era.
City: 12 consecutive PL games without an Arsenal win (Apr 2016–Oct 2023) — the longest such streak in this fixture's history. Included 11 City wins and 1 draw.

Goals Record

Most goals in a single match: 9 — Man City 6–3 Arsenal (Dec 2013).
Most Arsenal goals in a single match: 5 — both home (Oct 2000) and away (Feb 2003).
Most City goals in a single match: 6 — Dec 2013.

City's Absence from the PL

Man City were relegated from the Premier League after 1995–96 and missed four seasons (1996–2000). They returned for 2000–01 but were relegated again, missing 2001–02. From 2002-03 onwards they have been a permanent PL fixture. Their 9 seasons of absence means the fixture has fewer matches than most top-6 rivalries.

👔 The Manager Rivalry: Arteta vs Guardiola

The current incarnation of this rivalry has a remarkable backstory. Pep Guardiola and Mikel Arteta both came through Barcelona's La Masia academy — Arteta's debut for Barcelona B was as a substitute for Guardiola, whose career was winding down. They stayed in touch for decades.

When Guardiola joined City in 2016, he appointed Arteta as his assistant coach. Arteta worked alongside Guardiola for three years, winning two PL titles, an FA Cup and two League Cups. In December 2019, Arteta left to become Arsenal head coach.

"I would prefer to do it with someone else, to be fair. I want the best for him, genuinely, and when you are challenging with someone like this and something comes in-between that, it's a strange feeling."
— Mikel Arteta on competing against Pep Guardiola, 2023
